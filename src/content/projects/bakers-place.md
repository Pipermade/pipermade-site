Bakers Place comes from Luke's work in commercial carpentry. As senior carpenter and foreman for the build team on this Madison mixed-use building, he led the crew that made its millwork — shown here with the owners' permission.

The work runs through the building's shared spaces: the reception desk and coffered ceiling in the lobby, the full wall of built-in shelving in the amenity library, the slat ceilings and panelling in the community room, the pine-lined sauna, and the wood that wraps the elevator lobbies. Different rooms, one standard — long runs fit and aligned by hand, worked to match across ash, pine, and black-stained oak.

It's the same handwork and the same eye that now go into every Pipermade project — carried across a whole building's worth of interiors.
