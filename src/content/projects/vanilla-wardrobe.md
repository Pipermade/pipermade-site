The Vanilla Wardrobe is one part of a whole apartment lined in pine. In the bedroom, a run of built-in wardrobes wraps the window on both sides, and the reveal beneath it becomes a slim desk — a place to sit and work, framed by the joinery.

The same hand runs through the rest of the rooms: full-height shelving built into the living wall, panelling that hides the doors, and a niche sized for the media. Everything is scribed to the walls and finished in place, so the millwork reads as part of the architecture rather than furniture set against it.

Measured, cut, and finished by hand — the quiet kind of built-in work that makes a room feel considered without ever announcing itself.
